Mother Teresa

The Life of a Saint
Mother teresa

    Here’s a timeline of Mother Teresa:

  • 1910 - Mother Teresa is born in Skopje, now part of Macedonia.
  • 1928 - Joins the Sisters of Loreto in Ireland. There she learns English and is sent to the order’s girls’ school in Darjeeling, India, where she becomes a teacher, then principal.
  • 1946 - After leaving the school, she learns nursing skills from other nuns, and begins her work with Kolkata’s poor.
  • 1950 - Wins church recognition for a new order, the Missionaries of Charity, and becomes its superior general.
  • 1979 - Wins the Nobel Peace Prize for her work among the poor.
  • 1980 - Visits a Missionaries of Charity house in the Bronx.
  • 1982 - Travels to Beirut, rescuing dozens of developmentally disabled children from the fighting there.
  • 1990 - Suffering from heart problems, Mother Teresa tells Pope John Paul II she will resign as head of her order to make way for younger hands. But the order cannot agree on a successor, and she stays on.
  • 1997 - In failing health, she steps aside and is replaced by Sister Nirmala.
  • 1997 - Dies in Kolkata of heart failure. She was 87.
